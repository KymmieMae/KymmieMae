## What this is

I've spent 30 years in the construction industry — drafting, design, documentation, compliance, project coordination, management, and sales.

I'm now exploring a question that I think matters: how does AI actually change the way this industry works? Not in theory. In practice. Across the full project lifecycle — from the design office to the back office to site.

This is where I document that exploration. Follow along if it's useful to you.

---

## The question I'm sitting with

Construction is one of the most operationally complex industries on the planet. There's a project delivery pipeline running at all times — design, estimating, procurement, subcontracts, variations, progress claims, certifications, handover — while finance, compliance and business development run concurrently underneath it.

Most of that still runs on manual processes, disconnected platforms, and tribal knowledge.

AI is starting to change that. But the people building the tools often don't know the industry. And the people who know the industry often haven't yet found their way into the tools.

That gap is what I'm curious…. and excited about!

---

## The value of 30 years' experience in a world of AI

You cannot successfully automate a workflow that you do not deeply understand.

My perspective isn't built on software demos or theoretical structures; it's shaped by three decades of navigating the real-world friction of building projects. I understand the actual, practical mechanics that dictate whether a project runs smoothly or stalls.

I bring a deep, lived understanding of the critical dependencies between:

**Operations & Administration:** How office processes, contract variations, and documentation flow directly impact physical progress on-site.

**Compliance & Cash Flow:** Navigating complex regulatory frameworks (like the NCC, QDC, SEPP, and R-Codes) and the financial realities that keep subcontractors moving.

**The Human Element:** Construction is ultimately driven by relationships, and any new system must support — not disrupt — the way people actually communicate. Construction industry professionals already carry an enormous cognitive load. The goal of any AI implementation should be to give them back the parts of their day that are grinding them down — so they can focus on the work that actually requires their expertise, their relationships, and their judgment. That's not a threat to the industry. That's a better version of it.

This practical context is what allows me to look at AI through a highly realistic lens. I am not interested in tech for its own sake; I am focused on finding simple, practical ways to eliminate the real administrative friction that slows builders down.

## My AI philosophy

It's a new world and the new tools are genuinely exciting. I've been 
completely mesmerised by what's possible with image and video creation 
— I've even put together some comedy songs along the way 
([@PlainVanylla](https://www.youtube.com/@PlainVanylla)). 
But then agentic AI arrived and everything started to get complicated 
very quickly.

So I went looking for simplicity.

I found it in a methodology that just clicked: instead of building 
complex agentic workflows with API calls flying in every direction, 
start with a structured folder system that holds context and 
instructions. It's portable. It's platform-agnostic. It can be 
understood and maintained by someone who isn't technical. And it works.

For construction companies specifically, my view is this: before 
building anything, look at where AI has already been built into the 
tools you're paying for. Gain productivity there first. Then start 
mapping your workflows, decide which area to tackle first, and build 
from there — simply, deliberately, and in a way your team can actually 
understand and use.

We need to learn, understand, apply, and ship — not sit in a classroom for
two years chasing a degree to catch up with a world that's already moved on

## What I'm building here

**A Three-Stage Framework:** For mapping, diagnosing, and automating complex construction operational workflows.

**The Native AI Reference Library:** Simple playbooks for leveraging AI already embedded in tools many builders are already paying for — Microsoft 365, Xero, and others — delivering real productivity gains for zero (or minimal) extra software spend.

**Prompt Architecture & Methodologies:** Structured guides for processing operational discovery and translating real-world workflows into practical automation logic.

**Decision Frameworks:** Pragmatic rubrics for what to automate, what to leave alone, and where critical human judgement must stay in the loop.

This repository is a living document. It will grow as I learn, build, and break things.
